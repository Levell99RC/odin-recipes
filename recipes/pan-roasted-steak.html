Pan Roasted Steak

Description

A deliciously seared, butter-basted, garlicy, herby, medium rare steak cooked to perfection.

Ingredients

  • A thick (>; 1.5 in thick) ribeye or sirlion/NY steak
  • 2 tbsp of butter (salted or unsalted)
  • 3 cloves of garlic, crushed
  • A sprig of rosemary
  • A spring of thyme
  • Salt and pepper
  • A thermometer to test for perfect doneness

Steps

  1. At least 45 min before cooking the steak, generously season the steak with salt on both sides and leave in refrigerator, uncovered.
  2. When it's time to cook, preheat a cast iron or stainless steel pan on high with some oil.
  3. Once the smoke is lightly smoking, add in the steak. Make sure to lay away to avoid oil splatter.
  4. Flip once every minute.
  5. After two minutes, add the butter, garlic, thyme, and rosemary to the pan.
  6. Begin basting the steak with the infused butter and oil. Keep flipping.
  7. Begin testing with the thermometer after 4 minutes. 120F - 125F for rare. 125F - 130F for medium rare. 130F - 135F for medium. 135F-140F for medium well. 140F+ for well done. If cooking a thick cut steak, pull off the pan a few degrees off desired doneness as carry-over cooking will finish it.
  8. Take it out of the pan and let rest for 5 minutes. Pour over the oil and butter from the pan.
  9. Serve and enjoy!
